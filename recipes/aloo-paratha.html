Aloo Paratha Recipe (Aloo Ka Paratha)

What is Aloo Ka Paratha

Aloo means “potato” and paratha means “flatbread” in Hindi, so the name is quite telling: aloo paratha is an unleavened whole wheat flatbread stuffed with a savory, spiced, mashed potato filling. Hailing from Punjab, India, this is one of my favorite breakfasts of all time! It also happens to be one of the first recipes I learned from my Punjabi mother-in-law, so it holds a special place in my heart. In Punjabi families, parathas are made with lots of love and affection, not to mention lots of ghee and homemade butter (makhan). If you lean towards a healthier lifestyle, you can easily opt to roast aloo paratha in oil and serve them with curd (plain yogurt) instead of butter. In this post, I’ll show you two different methods of rolling the potato parathas. There’s no need to be intimidated; this recipe is really quite simple!

How to make Aloo Paratha

Before you can make these delicious stuffed parathas, you need to prepare the potato stuffing and make the whole wheat dough.

Make Potato Stuffing

First, boil or steam 4 medium-sized potatoes in a pressure cooker, steamer or electric cooker. Add enough water in a 2-litre pressure cooker or pan to just barely cover the potatoes.

For cooking in a pressure cooker -On a medium heat, cook the potatoes in water for 3 to 4 whistles. When the pressure settles down naturally, remove the lid and drain the water very well. Allow the potatoes to cool enough to handle and then peel them.

For cooking in a pan –Cover the pan and cook potatoes in water till fork tender. For this method, it helps to peel the potatoes and chop them in 2 inch cubes before boiling because they will cook faster than whole potatoes. Using a colander, drain all the water and allow the potatoes to cool.

    To make Potato Stuffing for Paratha

  • Firstly, in a large bowl take 3 potatoes, 1 tsp ginger paste, 2 chilli, 2 tbsp coriander leaves and ½ tsp coriander seeds.
  • Further, add ¼ tsp ajwain, 1 tsp chilli powder, ½ tsp cumin powder, ½ tsp garam masala, ¾ tsp amchur and ½ tsp salt.
  • Mix well making sure all the ingredients are well combined.
  • Potato stuffing is ready.

    • Method to make Aloo Paratha

  • After setting the dough aside for 20 minutes, knead a little.
  • Now take out a ball sized dough and roll into a diameter of about 4 inches.
  • Place a ball shaped potato in the centre.
  • Start pleating by holding the edge and bring together in the middle and secure to remove excess dough.
  • Sprinkle some wheat flour and roll slightly thick.
  • Place the rolled paratha on the hot tawa and cook for a minute.
  • Further, when the base is partially cooked (after a minute), flip the paratha.
  • Further, brush with oil/ghee and press slightly. Flip again till it is cooked well once or twice on both the sides.
  • Finally, serve hot aloo paratha with sauce, raita or pickle.
